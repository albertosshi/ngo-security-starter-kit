# Compromised Account Response Playbook

Use this playbook when you suspect that an email, cloud, Slack, or admin account has been compromised.

## First 15 minutes

1. Do not delete evidence.
2. Change the account password from a trusted device.
3. Revoke active sessions if the platform allows it.
4. Enable or reset multi-factor authentication.
5. Check whether recovery email or phone settings were changed.
6. Notify the person responsible for security or operations.

## First hour

1. Review recent login activity.
2. Check email forwarding rules.
3. Check mailbox filters and auto-delete rules.
4. Check recently shared files.
5. Check messages sent from the account.
6. Identify whether sensitive information may have been accessed.

## Same day

1. Inform affected staff or volunteers.
2. Warn contacts if phishing messages were sent.
3. Reset passwords for connected services if needed.
4. Review other accounts that used the same password.
5. Document what happened.

## Information to record

- Account affected
- Date and time discovered
- Who discovered it
- What suspicious activity was observed
- What actions were taken
- What data may have been exposed
- Who was notified
- Follow-up actions

## After the incident

- Review why the compromise happened.
- Enable MFA if it was missing.
- Remove unused accounts.
- Train staff or volunteers on similar attacks.
- Schedule a follow-up access review.
